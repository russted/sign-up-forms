How Publishers Can Embrace the Future of Books Now

Read the free case study now

Learn from Inkling’s Founder and CEO Matt MacInnis why eBooks are not the future of digital content—and what publishers should do about it.

It is a truth universally acknowledged that the publishing industry currently faces an existential crisis. Between the rise of new power players like Amazon, the corresponding decline in brick-and-mortar distribution channels, and the explosion of mobile devices, publishers are in the midst of monumental change. And yet, with this great change come even greater opportunities for the business leaders that can reimagine what it means to create and produce content. Based on years of experience in the tech industry and working closely with the world’s top publishers, like Pearson and Elsevier, Matt MacInnis shares his observations and predictions for how publishers can thrive in the digital-first age.

This report will teach you:

  • Why publishers must develop new product models before new financial models
  • Five key assumptions about the future that publishers should build their business on
  • What publishing can learn from the meteoric rise of the software industry
  • The most important thing publishers can do now to thrive

About The Author Matt MacInnis is the Founder and CEO of Inkling. With a vision for reinventing how knowledge is shared, Matt is passionate about making Inkling the leading platform for how content is built, distributed and consumed. Prior to founding Inkling in 2009, Matt managed Apple’s expansion into international education markets. Since then, Matt and the team have grown Inkling from an iPad app into an end-to-end cloud publishing solution that is used by the world’s leading publishing companies, such as Pearson, McGraw-Hill, and Elsevier. Matt has a Computer Engineering degree from Harvard University.
