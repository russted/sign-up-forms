The Editor’s Guide to Digital Content Quality

Read the free case study now

Learn the latest best practices for ensuring digital content quality at scale and across outputs.

With the rise of digital content formats, the task of content quality now includes everything from eliminating editorial flaws such as missed punctuation to highly enhancing a title with intuitively navigable links and multimedia. While much remains to be done, there are still some simple steps you can take now that will make the process easier and more successful.

Based on years of experience working directly with the world’s top publishers, like Pearson, Elsevier, Lonely Planet, and Kaplan, to bring their title list to life in digital formats, this guide reveals the emerging best practices and production methods for ensuring content quality at scale, regardless of output.

This eBook will teach you:

  • Establish better internal standards for making digital content as polished as print
  • Test drive your content efficiently with a robust production spec
  • Proof your content with new methods designed with digital in mind
  • Treat content like code for better quality across all outputs and formats

About The Author A reading fanatic of both digital and print content, Miranda Gregory is the Manager of Content Quality at Inkling (and admittedly abhors typos). She joined Inkling over three years ago and continues to be excited about helping transform the printed page into a digital, interactive experience. As one of Inkling’s original content team members, she’s been an integral part of developing processes for digital content quality at scale, picking up some hard-won lessons along the way. With a B.A. in History from Stanford University, Miranda is passionate about education and publishing.
