Webinar: Build Dynamic Workflows for Dynamic Teams
Monday July 25, 2014 • 2 pm EDT

How to Create Flexible Content Production Workflows That Put People Over Process

The era of “desktop publishing” for fixed layouts is over. Now, users demand high-quality content that can flex to fit every device. Yet, these demands stretch current workflows to the limit, putting complicated processes ahead of your most valuable content production resource: your people.

Enter cloud publishing. Through output-agnostic workflows that employ professional-grade tools designed for the non-technical user, cloud publishing will put your content creators and managers back in charge.

Based on years of working with the world’s top publishers and content creators to develop workflow solutions that fit their digital content production needs, Inkling’s Director of Client Solutions Nolan Myers shares how the new cloud publishing-based workflow will empower your teams to create and collaborate on high-quality digital content at scale.

You'll learn how to:

  • Design content workflows that can flex with any output or team size
  • Leverage your teams more effectively with cloud publishing tools that put people first
  • Recognize the five key indicators of a healthy, people-centric content production workflow
  • Reduce time to market for digital content and quickly update content across outputs
  • Empower authors and content creators to be more creative and innovative without fear of “breaking things”

About The Speaker:

Nolan Myers is Inkling’s Director of Client Solutions. Nolan led Inkling’s content production from ten titles a year to hundreds per month. He and his team now help Inkling’s partners leverage its cloud publishing technologies and processes to build beautiful content efficiently. Having been in the ed-tech and publishing industry for more than a decade, Nolan is also the co-holder of several pending U.S. patents on workflow technologies for building and publishing digital content. He holds a B.A. in Computer Science from Harvard University.
